# Chest Pain Guidance

Chest pain can be caused by cardiac and non-cardiac conditions. Potential serious causes
include acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, aortic syndromes, and severe pneumonia.

Risk increases with older age, cardiovascular history, diabetes, smoking, and high blood pressure.

In triage settings, chest pain accompanied by sweating, shortness of breath, syncope,
or radiation to jaw/arm usually requires urgent physician or emergency evaluation.
